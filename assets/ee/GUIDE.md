# Desert Chronicles — Field Guide & Walkthrough

*Contains full spoilers, including the final answer and all three endings.*

June 1986. You are Professor James Walker, retired archaeologist. The morning
paper says a grading crew has cut into an ancient canal near the airport, and a
reader's letter claims a mountain carving shows an exploding star — a carving
you sketched yourself in 1956 and never understood. The solstice is June 21.
The concrete pours June 23. Fill your canteen.

---

## The rules of the desert

- **Time runs.** One real minute is one game hour. The clock (bottom of the
  screen) shows the date and counts down to the solstice. Some things can only
  be seen at **dawn (5–8 AM)**, some at **dusk (5–8 PM)**, one at **noon**, and
  one at **night**.
- **Camp to control time.** Campfires offer *Sleep until dawn* (+20 HP) or
  *Wait for dusk* (+10 HP). Both cost a little water. This is how you make
  appointments with the sun.
- **Water is life.** Hydration drains constantly, brutally so outdoors between
  **11 AM and 4 PM** — travel at dawn and dusk like the sign says. Refill at
  any water source; staying above half hydration slowly heals you.
- **Listen before you look.** A rattlesnake buzzes before it strikes. Freeze or
  back away and it settles; press in and you get bitten — venom drains health
  slowly until cured by a **prickly pear** (which also heals 35 HP; you can
  carry five).
- **Read the wildlife.** A roadrunner working the ground means the snakes there
  stay calm. Hawks wheeling in a tight circle mark a cache below. And if a
  coyote walks ahead of you and keeps looking back — follow at a distance and
  see where you end up.
- **Press M** for Walker's field map. It tracks the three records you carry and
  draws each alignment in gold as you witness it.
- **Check the Model 100** in your satchel — Walker's pocket computer shows the
  date and time, the dawn/dusk reading windows, the solstice countdown, your
  progress, and warns you if there's venom in your blood (a green **VENOM**
  badge also sits by the HP bar until you eat a prickly pear).
- **You can't really lose.** The game checkpoints on every map change, camp,
  and pickup. Death returns you to the last checkpoint.

## The levels, west to east

### 1. Sonoran Desert Outskirts — the road's end
Your truck is parked where the pavement gives up. **Ranger Rick** teaches the
survival rules, the **Old Prospector** asks you to fetch his pickaxe from the
mine (do it — his reward is the clue to the endgame: *"the ancients' door hides
behind the Phoenix; push what cannot fly"*), and the **UFO Watcher** wrote the
letter in the paper — remember him. Water, a campfire, and a certain coyote.
Exits: the canyon (east), Dusty Gulch (south).

### 2. Red Rock Canyon — the cache
A slot canyon with two routes: the shaded north corridor (a rattler waits at
the blind corner) and the dry wash to the south — fast, and the wrong place to
linger. In the central chamber a chest holds the **Calendar Stick Fragment
(record 1 of 3)**, cached in modern oilcloth. Talk to the **Old Hermit**
afterward: he watched a dying man bury it in March, and passes on his message —
*"the rest is where the water was."* At **noon** a blade of light stands on the
chamber floor (optional journal record). Past the wash, the hermit keeps a tin
stocked for travelers.

### 3. Camelback Mountain Trail — the climb
Switchbacks up three terraces. **Vance Cutler** waits at the trailhead —
charming, funny, and headed to the Superstitions after Dutchman gold; he'll
matter later. Mid-climb, a **Tired Hiker** is out of water: share yours if you
can spare it. On the summit ridge: the Praying Monk, Echo Canyon, a climber's
cache — and at **dawn**, the overlook shows the valley's peaks lighting up in
one line (optional record, and the game's first hint of what the whole valley
is).

### 4. Papago Park — Lesson 1: read a light
The red butte with the hole clean through it. Camp until dawn, then stand in
the chamber at **first light (5–8 AM)**: the sun crawls across the floor toward
a pecked basin. This is **alignment reading 1 of 3 — required**.

### 5. The Arizona Canal, Lock 9 — the crossing
1912 concrete on a nine-hundred-year-old grade. **Lock Keeper Vega**'s family
has always worked water here. Cross by the footbridge. Periodically a
**haboob** rolls in from the east and visibility collapses — get low, listen,
and steer for the pulsing ring of the wind chime; ducking into the lock house
waits it out over coffee.

### 6. Pueblo Grande Platform Mound — Lesson 2: read a doorway
Dr. Delgado's salvage dig, racing the June 23 pour. Under a tarp near the
looter's pit: the **Etched Olla Shard (record 2 of 3)**. Then camp until dawn
and stand in the doorway atop the mound: it frames Hole-in-the-Rock across
twelve miles of valley. **Alignment reading 2 of 3 — required.** If you let
June 23 pass, the concrete comes — the map changes, and Delgado saves the
shard for you at a cost.

### 7. Casa Grande Ruins — the Great House
Four stories of caliche under a steel canopy, its wall ports drilled for the
sun, the moon, and Venus. At **dusk** the round port lights up (optional
record). Ranger Alvarez says the important thing plainly: *"Ask the O'odham
down the road. Nobody went anywhere."*

### 8. South Mountain Petroglyphs — Lesson 3: read a horizon
**Frances Antone**, Salt River, hydrology. She corrects you — *Huhugam* means
the ancestors, and the people never stopped — and decides what you've earned.
Camp until dusk, then watch from the worn spot below the notch: the sun settles
into the ridge. **Alignment reading 3 of 3 — required.** Mind the carved
guardians; they shoot.

### 9. White Tank Mountains — the star
The star-scorpion panel: a bursting star beside Scorpius, exactly your 1956
sketch. Reading it makes the **Star Chart Rubbing (record 3 of 3)** and anchors
the whole calendar to **AD 1006** — remember that number. At night, lie on the
granite shelf and watch Scorpius rise (optional record). Tinaja water, and the
scorched proof that the "UFO" was a flare.

### 10–11. Dusty Gulch & the Copper Mine — the side trip
A ghost town (Anglo ghosts only, and they know it) and the mine below it. Fetch
the **Prospector's pickaxe** from the mine and return it for the Phoenix clue.
A lucky rabbit foot hides down there too — it makes a good gift, later.

### 12. Superstition Mountains — the control group
East of Dusty Gulch, the hardest ground in the game: javelina pairs, rattlers,
a gila monster. Cutler's camp is here, flags and generator and a genuinely
persuasive offer. Fight through to the marked spot the legend names and find
what every Dutchman hunter finds: **an old empty pit and a rusted canteen.**
That's the point. The desert doesn't curse anyone; it just doesn't pay out.

### 13. ASU — Hayden Archives and Lab
The **Grad Student** hands you the computer club's **UV lamp** (at night or
underground it reveals glowing scorpions — and painted traces on the rocks at
White Tanks, the mine, and the canyon). The mainframe loads the solstice
ephemeris; the microfiche finds the one canal segment outside the grading
permit. Behind the **Phoenix statue** ("push what cannot fly") is the panel:
it opens only when you carry **all three records** and have witnessed **all
three alignment readings**, and tells you what's missing if you interact early.

### 14. The Vault Beneath the Canal Line — the finale
Down the maintenance stair. Before the sealed olla will let you reach for it,
stop at the **offering ledge** by the stair and leave something —
**permanently**: the rabbit foot, the rusted canteen, the old pickaxe, a
prickly pear, or a pour of your own water. You don't take from these places
without giving.

Then touch the olla. If the solstice hasn't come, Walker banks a fire and
**waits** — the one morning the whole instrument was built to catch. At first
light on **June 21**, the light comes down the stair, and the seal asks its
question.

## How to win

**The short version:** collect the three records, witness the three dawn/dusk
readings, open the ASU panel, leave a gift, wait for the solstice, answer the
seal, and choose what Walker does with four centuries.

1. **Three records** (tracked on the field map):
   - *Calendar Stick Fragment* — chest, Red Rock Canyon.
   - *Etched Olla Shard* — under the tarp, Pueblo Grande (Delgado holds it for
     you if the pour beats you there).
   - *Star Chart Rubbing* — star panel, White Tanks.
2. **Three readings** (camp at the fire, then be standing there):
   - Dawn light at Hole-in-the-Rock (Papago, 5–8 AM).
   - The mound doorway at dawn (Pueblo Grande, 5–8 AM).
   - The horizon notch at dusk (South Mountain, 5–8 PM).
3. **ASU secret panel** behind the Phoenix statue → the vault.
4. **Leave an offering** at the ledge.
5. **The seal's question** — *"The great star carved beside the star-scorpion.
   When did it blaze?"* — the answer is **AD 1006** (it's in your 1956
   notebook and on the rubbing). Wrong answers aren't fatal; Cutler needles
   you and the seal lets you try again.
6. **The choice.** Cutler makes his offer, and the game asks what Walker does:
   - **THE SPLIT** — sell it. Comfortable. Smaller than the desert.
   - **THE PLAQUE** — the museum and your name on the paper. Frances doesn't
     call back.
   - **THE RETURN** — one phone call: Salt River, cultural office, Frances
     Antone's desk. *Some things aren't found, Professor. They're returned.*

None of the endings is a game over. One of them is right.

### The completionist's journal
Beyond the required path: the Prospector's pickaxe, the hermit's blessing, the
canyon noon-blade, the Camelback dawn sightline, the shared water on the
mountain, the Great House Venus port, Scorpius rising, the Dutchman bust, and
three UV traces (White Tanks pigment, the miners' chalk, the spiral's painted
tail). A finished journal has every line crossed out — including the first one.
